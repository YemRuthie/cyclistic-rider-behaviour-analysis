SCRIPT 05 - MEMBER VS CASUAL DURATION
=================================================

Objective:
Compare ride duration between members and casual riders.

Pre-Analysis Hypothesis:

Members may use Cyclistic primarily for commuting and transportation purposes.

Casual riders may use Cyclistic for leisure and recreational activities.

If this assumption is correct, casual riders should have longer ride durations than members.

Method:
Grouped cleaned data by member_casual and calculated:
- Count
- Mean
- Median
- Min
- Max

Results:

Casual Riders
Count: 235,846
Mean Ride Length: 19.69 minutes
Median Ride Length: 11.81 minutes

Members
Count: 399,516
Mean Ride Length: 12.43 minutes
Median Ride Length: 8.87 minutes

Outcome:

The hypothesis was supported.

Casual riders take significantly longer trips than members.

The average casual ride is approximately 58% longer than the average member ride.

Business Interpretation:

Member behaviour suggests commuting and utility-based travel.

Casual rider behaviour suggests leisure and recreational use.

SCRIPT 07 - BIKE TYPE ANALYSIS
=================================================

Objective:
Compare bike type usage between members and casual riders.

Pre-Analysis Hypothesis:

Members may prefer electric bikes because they are more likely to use Cyclistic for transportation and commuting purposes.

Electric bikes may provide greater convenience and require less physical effort.

Casual riders may show greater use of classic bikes because recreational riders may value the cycling experience itself and may be more likely to use bikes on scenic or leisure-focused trips.

Method:
Grouped rides by:
- member_casual
- rideable_type

Calculated:
- Ride count
- Approximate usage percentages

Results:

Members

Electric Bike: 270,041 rides
Classic Bike: 129,475 rides

Electric Usage Rate: 67.6%
Classic Usage Rate: 32.4%

Casual Riders

Electric Bike: 169,932 rides
Classic Bike: 65,914 rides

Electric Usage Rate: 72.0%
Classic Usage Rate: 28.0%

Outcome:

The hypothesis was not supported.

Both rider groups showed a strong preference for electric bikes.

Unexpectedly, casual riders used electric bikes slightly more frequently than members.

Business Interpretation:

Electric bikes appear to be attractive to both rider groups.

Convenience and ease of use may be important factors regardless of rider type.

However, usage alone does not prove preference.

Bike availability, station distribution, and fleet composition may also influence bike selection.

=================================================
CURRENT INSIGHTS
=================================================

1. The dataset is large and suitable for behavioural analysis, containing 653,704 ride records.

2. Data cleaning was necessary due to implausible ride durations, including rides lasting less than one minute and rides exceeding 24 hours.

3. A total of 18,342 anomalous records were removed, leaving 635,362 rides for analysis.

4. Ride duration data is right-skewed. Most rides are relatively short, with a median duration of approximately 10 minutes; however, a smaller number of longer rides contribute to the overall average.

5. Casual riders take significantly longer trips than members.
   - Casual average ride length: 19.69 minutes
   - Member average ride length: 12.43 minutes

6. The hypothesis that casual riders take longer rides than members was supported.

7. Casual riders show strong weekend usage patterns, particularly on Saturdays.

8. The hypothesis that casual riders are more active on weekends was supported.

9. Members demonstrate consistently high usage throughout the week rather than a strong weekday-only pattern.

10. The hypothesis that members primarily ride during weekdays was only partially supported.

11. Electric bikes are the most frequently used bike type among both rider groups.

12. The hypothesis that casual riders may prefer classic bikes was not supported.

13. Casual riders use electric bikes slightly more frequently than members when measured as a percentage of total rides.

14. The analysis so far suggests that casual riders are more leisure-oriented, while members integrate Cyclistic more consistently into their regular transportation habits.

15. Members display strong morning and evening commuting peaks, particularly at 08:00 and 17:00.

16. The hour-of-day analysis provides strong evidence that members use Cyclistic as part of their regular transportation routine.

17. Casual riders display a more gradual usage pattern throughout the day, consistent with leisure and discretionary travel.
